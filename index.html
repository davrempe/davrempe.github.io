Davis Rempe - About Me

I finished my undergraduate work at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln in December, 2016 with a B.S. in Computer Science and Mathematics as well as a minor in Physics. I also took many classes within the film school that have given me experience in digital graphics and visual effects (especially with Adobe After Effects), and computer animation (especially with Autodesk Maya). My main research interest is computer graphics, but I find many topics in computer vision, and data visualization interesting too. Within computer graphics, I'm most intrigued by physically-based simulation and modeling, especially related to computer animation and visual effects, and plan on attending graduate school to research these topics or a closely related area.

Outside of school I enjoy traveling whenever possible, and I have blogged about most of my international travel. So far I've been to the Czech Republic (where I studied abroad), Austria, France, Vietnam, and Canada, but I'm always looking to expand this list. I'm also a big soccer fan, and love attending every home Nebrasketball game.

This site contains some projects that I've found most interesting over my undergraduate career, along with publications, and my CV.
